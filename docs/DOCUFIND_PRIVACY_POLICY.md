# DocuFind Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 2, 2026

DocuFind is a local-first Android app for storing, organizing, previewing, and remembering important personal documents, family records, pet records, IDs, cards, warranties, prescriptions, insurance details, vehicle records, and reminders.

This Privacy Policy explains how DocuFind accesses, stores, uses, protects, shares, retains, and deletes information when you use the DocuFind Android app.

## Summary

DocuFind is designed to keep your information on your device.

- We do not operate a server that stores your documents or app data.
- We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information.
- We do not use advertising SDKs.
- We do not use third-party analytics or behavioral tracking.
- Your documents, notes, reminders, attachments, photos, and app settings are stored locally on your device.
- Files are shared, exported, backed up, or opened outside DocuFind only when you choose to do so.

## Information DocuFind may access or store

DocuFind may access or store the following information only for app functionality:

### Documents and attachments

You may add files, images, PDFs, scanned documents, IDs, prescriptions, reports, certificates, invoices, warranties, insurance documents, vehicle records, pet documents, family photos, and other attachments.

Purpose: to let you store, preview, organize, search, share, and manage records inside the app.

Storage: app-private local device storage.

### Record details and metadata

You may enter titles, categories, document types, issue dates, expiry dates, notes, ID references, issuer names, card last four digits, policy details, medical details, prescription details, pet details, family member details, emergency contact details, and similar information.

Purpose: to organize records, show category details, power local search, and create reminders.

Storage: local device database.

### Photos and camera captures

DocuFind may access the camera only when you choose to scan a document or take a profile/photo attachment.

Purpose: to let you capture a document, family photo, pet photo, or attachment.

Storage: app-private local device storage after you save the record.

### Files selected from your device

DocuFind may access files that you explicitly select from your device using Android file pickers.

Purpose: to attach selected files to your records.

Storage: copied into app-private local storage so previews and records do not depend only on the original external file location.

### Reminder and notification data

DocuFind stores reminder titles, dates, due dates, snooze/action status, category links, and notification scheduling details.

Purpose: to remind you about renewals, expiries, medicines, vaccinations, warranties, prescriptions, insurance, vehicle documents, and custom reminders.

Storage: local device database and local Android notification scheduling.

### Local app activity insights

If Activity Insights is available, DocuFind may store local-only usage/activity information such as app opens, vault opens, screen counts, search usage counts, files stored counts, documents added over time, reminder completion summaries, and storage usage.

Purpose: to show private, local reports inside the app.

Storage: encrypted local device database only. Activity events do not store document titles, notes, file names, search query text, decrypted metadata, or attachment content.

### Support diagnostics

If you contact support from within the app, DocuFind may prepare basic diagnostic information such as app version, Android version, device model, and timestamp.

Purpose: to help troubleshoot problems.

Documents, databases, encrypted files, PINs, backup passwords, sensitive record contents, and file names are not automatically attached to support requests.

## Permissions used by DocuFind

DocuFind requests permissions only when needed for app functionality.

### Camera

Used when you choose to scan a document or capture a photo. DocuFind does not access the camera in the background.

### Notifications

Used to send reminder notifications for due dates, renewals, medicines, vaccinations, warranties, and custom reminders. On Android 13 and later, DocuFind asks for notification permission before sending notifications.

### Device storage / file picker access

Used through Android system file pickers when you choose a file, choose where to save a backup, or choose a backup file to restore. DocuFind does not scan your device storage automatically.

### Biometric authentication

Used only if you enable biometric unlock. Biometric data is handled by the Android system and is not collected, stored, or transmitted by DocuFind.

## How information is used

DocuFind uses information for the following purposes:

- Store and organize your records.
- Display and preview attachments.
- Create, update, and complete reminders.
- Search your locally stored records.
- Protect access with PIN and biometric unlock where enabled.
- Create encrypted backups when you choose to export a backup.
- Restore encrypted backups when you choose to import one.
- Show local-only activity insights and storage summaries where available.
- Help troubleshoot issues if you contact support.

## Sharing and disclosure

DocuFind does not sell your personal or sensitive information.

DocuFind does not share your documents or personal information with third parties automatically.

Information may leave the app only when you take an action such as:

- Sharing a record or attachment.
- Opening a file in another app.
- Exporting or saving an encrypted backup.
- Sending support information by email.
- Restoring or importing a file you choose.

When you share, open, export, or back up data, the receiving app, storage provider, email provider, or destination you choose may process that information under its own privacy practices.

## Data security

DocuFind uses local-first security measures designed to protect sensitive records:

- App-private storage for uploaded files and attachments.
- Local database storage for records and reminders.
- PIN and biometric access controls for protected areas where enabled.
- Screenshot blocking on sensitive screens where supported.
- Encrypted backup files when you choose to create a backup.
- No advertising SDKs.
- No third-party analytics SDKs.
- No cloud document sync operated by DocuFind.

No method of storage or transmission is completely risk-free. You are responsible for protecting your device, device lock screen, backup files, and backup passwords.

## Backup and restore

If you create a backup, DocuFind creates an encrypted backup file using a password you provide.

DocuFind cannot recover your backup password. If you lose the backup password, the encrypted backup may not be recoverable.

Backup files are saved only to the location you choose, such as your device storage or a storage provider selected through Android system UI.

## Data retention

DocuFind keeps your records, attachments, reminders, settings, and local insights on your device until you delete them, clear app data, uninstall the app, or restore/replace data from a backup.

DocuFind does not retain a server copy of your documents or personal data because DocuFind does not operate server-side document storage.

## Data deletion

You can delete records, attachments, reminders, family records, pet records, and other stored items from within the app where deletion is available.

You can also delete all local DocuFind data by uninstalling the app or clearing app storage through Android system settings.

If you created backup files outside the app, you must delete those backup files from the storage location where you saved them.

## Attachment previews

When you add a supported file, DocuFind copies it into app-private storage and encrypts it for local use. Preview, Open, and Share actions decrypt a temporary copy only when you choose that action and have unlocked the protected area.

Opening or sharing sends the temporary file only to the app or destination you select through Android system UI. If a legacy or missing attachment can no longer be accessed, DocuFind shows a safe unavailable-file message instead of crashing.

## Search and sensitive fields

DocuFind's local search index is derived from non-sensitive record metadata such as title, category, family/pet association, dates, and user-created tags. Sensitive category fields such as ID numbers, policy numbers, card numbers, passwords, and secure notes are encrypted and are not copied into the search cache.

## Children

DocuFind is intended as a productivity and personal records app for general audiences. It is not directed to children. Do not use DocuFind to collect or store information about children unless you have the legal right to do so.

## International users

Your data is stored locally on your device unless you choose to share, export, back up, or open it using another app or service. If you use third-party apps or cloud storage services, your data may be processed according to those services' terms and privacy policies.
